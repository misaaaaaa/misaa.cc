Instructive

  • For executing it, you should go to a subway station with your mobile, and over-ear headphones (If you don’t have them, you can use in-ear too). The lockdowns should be loose enough, at least, to have access to public transport.
  • Go to a place of the station, where you are at least at a medium distance of steady sound sources, like turnstiles, platforms and loudspeakers; try not have them on the first "plane". Search for a place where you can stand comfortably or sit in the floor, like if you were waiting for somebody or making time reading a book.
  • Connect the headphones to your mobile, put them over your ears.
  • Play the Test file. Try to level the volume (with the controls of your phone) to a mid level, where it doesn’t mask the soundscape of the subway.
    • Test file
  • When you are ready, put in a comfortable position, standing or sitting. Play Subway memories file. The music will start 1 minute after hitting Play, so you can get used first to the subway soundscape. Listen for the time you want or can. Then leave.


    • Próxima estación: Memoria



Alternative variation

  • Go to a subway station.
  • Try to get access (as you can) to the P.A. system. Probably is on the ticket booths (easier), or in the administration offices (maybe requires some extra permission).
  • Connect your mobile device. If you can’t find the way, play it from your mobile to one of the microphones.
  • Maybe you will not have time to play the testfile, so you should play this file with no silence at the beginning.

Work's description

I have a strange memory from my childhood. In the 90’s, when I was like 5 or 6 years old, I was in a subway station with my mother. And I realised there was a calm ambient music surrounding us. I ask her why that music, and she told that the Metro of Santiago was really cool. Clean and few people used it, and they played that music to keep a vibe of peacefulness. It was a whole experience.

But in 2006, the soundscape changed forever. A bad implementation of the new infrastructure on the public transport called “Transantiago”, which changed all the buses numberings and their paths, turned this calm place into a chaotic one, because most of the people began to move on rails, instead of this unknown buses system. At one point, they started to put TV’s on the subway platform, changed the pay system to one which makes a “beep” when you cross the turnstiles, workers who had to yell to keep people in order… so the experience of traveling across the city became so much more stressful.

I am researching about this ambient music on the 90’s, and just few people can remember it. The metro doesn’t have public information about this time, and which were the composers of the music. I couldn’t find yet a sound recording of this time to confirm my memories. So this work is a sonic-reconstruction about those sound experience, which were forgotten because of the urban and social progression.

Artist bio

Matías Serrano is a Sound Artist, who born and live in Santiago, Chile. He holds a Bachelor in Arts degree in Sound from the University of Chile. Explores the sounds of our enviroments, technologies, and gender/class issues of society. Works since 2015 in Colectiva 22bits, dedicated to explore the borders of sound, design, art & technology, with a technofeminist perspective. There, developes hardware instruments, make workshops, exhibitions and concerts mainly through latin america. Manages the Archivo Veintidós netlabel, which publish sound artists & experimental musicians from latin america. As a composer, under the name of misaa, released his first album on 2019, and publish on his webpage a collection of urban & nature soundscapes. He works too on film and visual arts projects, collaborating with artists, directors and institutions.
